Pillar IV

Hybrid Warfare

RAW operations. BLA. TTP as state-proxy post-2021. Af-Pak zone-extension. Information warfare. Which domestic media formations operate in the hybrid war space.

Core Argument

Pakistan's post-2001 security environment is read here as a single hybrid-war campaign conducted across multiple simultaneous vectors — externally sponsored separatist violence, a post-2021 TTP that functions as a cross-border state proxy rather than an autonomous insurgency, and an information-warfare layer that recruits domestic political and media actors into narratives originating outside the country. This pillar maps those vectors as one coordinated field rather than as unrelated security incidents, and identifies which domestic formations knowingly or unknowingly operate inside it.

The War on Terror as Hidden Target

The pressure this pillar documents did not begin with the post-2021 Taliban return to Kabul. GP-009 argues that the War on Terror period placed sustained external pressure on the garrison state itself, with the counter-terrorism campaign's stated objectives masking pressure applied to Pakistan's own ideological and institutional continuity.

Six Vectors, One Map

GP-019 is this pillar's central document — it lays out six distinct vectors of attack on Pakistan's ideological existence and argues they should be read as a single hybrid-war campaign rather than six unconnected problems. The anti-institutional mobilization documented in GP-007 is treated as this pillar's clearest case of a domestic political formation operating inside that same information-warfare space, whatever its own actors' intent.
